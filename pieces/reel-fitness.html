The Gym Was Never Built for People Like Us

Category
Scripts · Personal Branding
Format
Reel script, founder-story (33 seconds)
Audience
People who feel unwelcome in traditional gym spaces
Tone
Raw, unproduced, mildly defiant

Editor's note: Written to turn a weight-loss story into a brand origin story without making the brand the hero. The anger in the body section is the actual selling point, not the weight lost.

[HOOK — 0:00 to 0:04]

(No music. No intro. Asmita straight to camera, zero performance.)

I lost 22 kilos and walked into a gym maybe four times. So, can we please stop pretending it is the only way to get results?

[BODY — 0:05 to 0:24]

(Raw cuts. Asmita's living room. No matching set, no ring light, no production.)

Every time I walked into a gym, I felt watched. Judged. Like everyone in that room could see exactly how far I had to go.

The stares. The mirrors are everywhere. The feeling of being the biggest, the slowest, the most out of place person in the building.

So I never went back after the first few tries. Then I got angry. Not motivated, not inspired. Just genuinely angry about how I felt in my own body every single day.

That anger is what built Lift by Asmita.

No equipment. No programme I paid a fortune for. Just floor space, YouTube, and just me trying to feel better in a body I had stopped being kind to.

The weight came off slowly at first. Then steadily. Then it just stayed gone.

Because I stopped waiting until I was comfortable enough to walk into a room full of people who already looked the way I was trying to look.

[CLOSE — 0:25 to 0:33]

(Still. Direct. No forced smile. Just Asmita.)

The gym did not fail you. It was just never designed for people like us.

Comment 'lift,' and I will send you exactly how I started from zero, at home, with nothing but a decision that I was done feeling that way.
